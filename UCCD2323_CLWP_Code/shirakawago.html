About Shirakawa-go

Shirakawa-go is a UNESCO World Heritage Site famous for its traditional gassho-zukuri farmhouses, some of which are more than 250 years old. These unique houses are designed to withstand heavy snowfall with their steep thatched roofs.

Key Attractions

  • Ogimachi Village - the largest village and main attraction
  • Shiroyama Viewpoint - panoramic view of the village
  • Local cuisine - try the Hida beef and sansai (mountain vegetables)
  • Winter illumination (January-February)

Travel Information

Location: Gifu Prefecture, Central Japan

Transport: ~2.5 hours by bus from Takayama or Kanazawa

Best Time to Visit: All seasons (especially winter for snow scenery)

UNESCO Designation: 1995
