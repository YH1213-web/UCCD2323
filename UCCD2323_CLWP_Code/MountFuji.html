Mount Fuji

Mount Fuji, providing a spectacular fusion of nature and serenity. In the spring, the cherry blossoms bloom, highlighting the peak of Mount Fuji. In summer, it is green and lush, and in autumn, the forests surrounding the mountain are transformed into a tapestry of red, orange, and gold. In winter, the peak sparkles with snow against a crisp blue sky. Whether seen in the quiet waters of Lake Kawaguchi or from the nearby rolling tea fields of Shizuoka, the beauty of Mount Fuji is haunting to behold.

Key Attractions:

Mount Fuji Summit: At an elevation of 3,776 meters, Mount Fuji's peak gives stunning panoramic views. The experience of climbing to the top is rewarding, especially when attempted during the official climbing season in the summer.

Chureito Pagoda: A famous five-story pagoda that has a stunning view of Mount Fuji, especially during cherry blossom season. It's a perfect place to capture the iconic image of Japan's natural beauty.

Lake Kawaguchi and Aokighara Forest: Lake Kawaguchi is one of the Fuji Five Lakes, this scenic lake offers picturesque reflections of Mount Fuji on clear days. Visitors can enjoy boat rides, hot springs, and seasonal flower festivals.

Seasonal Highlights

Spring

Offers a breathtaking sight, especially during the cherry blossom season (sakura) from March to April, offering a beautiful contrast of pink blossoms against the mountain's snow-capped peaks

Summer

Mount Fuji's official climbing season (July-September) offers a unique experience, with the mountain often snow-free and accessible, but visibility can be poor due to cloud cover, and temperatures can be significantly cooler at higher altitudes

Autumn

Offers a stunning visual experience with vibrant foliage and comfortable weather, making it a popular time for viewing the iconic mountain. The Fuji Five Lakes area, particularly Lake Kawaguchi, is a great spot to enjoy this scenery

Winter

Offers a breathtaking, snow-covered landscape and clear skies, making it a popular destination for photography and nature enthusiasts, though climbing is not permitted during this season

How to Get There

To reach Mount Fuji by train from Tokyo, take the JR Chuo Line express train to Otsuki Station, then transfer to the privately-run Fuji Kyuko Line (Fujikyu Railway) to Kawaguchiko Station, which is a gateway to the Fuji Five Lakes region.

To reach Mount Fuji by bullet train (Shinkansen), take the Tokaido Shinkansen from Tokyo Station or Shinagawa Station to Shin-Fuji Station, then transfer to a local bus or train to reach the Fuji area.

To reach Mount Fuji by bus from Tokyo, take a highway bus from Shinjuku Bus Terminal (Busta Shinjuku) to Kawaguchiko Station or Fuji-Q Highland, which takes about 2-2.5 hours and costs around 2,000 yen.

To climb Mount Fuji by foot, you'll need to start at one of the four 5th stations (halfway up the mountain), and the most popular route is the Yoshida Trail, accessible from the Fuji-Subaru Line 5th Station.
