Fushimi Inari Taisha

Fushimi Inari Taisha is Kyoto's most important Shinto shrine and famous for its thousands of vermilion torii gates, which straddle a network of trails behind its main buildings. The trails lead into the wooded forest of the sacred Mount Inari, which stands at 233 meters.

Key Attractions

Senbon Torii: The iconic pathway of thousands of closely spaced orange torii gates that form tunnels along the hiking trails up Mount Inari.

Main Shrine Complex: Features the Romon Gate, donated in 1589 by Toyotomi Hideyoshi, and the main hall where visitors can pay respects to the deity Inari.

Seasonal Highlights

Spring

Cherry blossoms add delicate pink accents to the vibrant orange gates from late March to early April.

Summer

Lush greenery surrounds the torii gates, creating a cool, shaded walk. Early morning visits are recommended.

Autumn

The maple trees turn brilliant red and orange from November to early December.

Winter

Snow occasionally dusts the gates, creating a magical atmosphere. The shrine is beautifully illuminated during winter evenings.

How to Get There

From Kyoto Station, take the JR Nara Line to Inari Station (5 minutes). The shrine is just across from the station.

Take the Keihan Main Line to Fushimi-Inari Station (about 5 minutes from Gion-Shijo Station). The shrine entrance is a 3-minute walk.

Kyoto City Bus routes 5, 105, and others stop at "Inari Taisha-mae" bus stop, about a 7-minute walk to the shrine.

From central Kyoto (Gion area), it's about a 30-minute walk along scenic streets to reach the shrine's main entrance.
