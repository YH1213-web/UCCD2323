Tokyo Tower

Welcome to Tokyo Tower ! One of Japan's most iconic landmarks! Standing proudly at 333 meters, this striking red and white structure offers breathtaking views of Tokyo's skyline. Whether you're here for the stunning observation decks, cultural exhibits or simply to admire its architectural beauty, Tokyo Tower is a must-visit destination that blends history, innovation and unforgettable experiences.

Key Attractions:

Main Deck: Located at 150 meters, this deck provides a fantastic view of the city, including iconic landmarks like the Tokyo Skytree

Illuminations : At night, Tokyo Tower transforms into a luminous spectacle with "Diamond Veil" and "Landmark Light" illuminations.

Roof Garden: A tranquil haven atop Tokyo Tower, where the roof garden offers a serene reprieve from the bustling city below.

Seasonal Highlights

Spring

Adorned with vibrant koinobori (carp streamers) for Boys' Day and features a cherry blossom light show, creating a festive and visually stunning atmosphere

Summer

Landmark light illumination shifts to a silver color (metal-halide lamps) from its usual orange color and the tower's summer version light show begins around July 7th, coinciding with Tanabata and continues until early October.

Autumn

Surrounding Shiba Koen park transforms with vibrant fall foliage, featuring maple and ginkgo trees turning shades of red, orange and yellow, creating a picturesque backdrop for the iconic tower.

Winter

A magical winter wonderland with its iconic "Landmark Light" illumination, a warm orange glow that creates a festive atmosphere, and special events like "Tokyo Tower Winter Fantasy" with light shows and decorations.

How to Get There

To reach Tokyo Tower by train, take the Oedo Line to Akabanebashi Station (5-minute walk), the Mita Line to Onarimon Station (6-minute walk), or the Hibiya Line to Kamiyacho Station (6-minute walk) or the JR Yamanote Line to Hamamatsucho Station (15-minute walk).

To reach Tokyo Tower by bullet train (Shinkansen), take a Shinkansen to Tokyo Station, then transfer to the JR Yamanote Line or the subway to Hamamatsucho Station which is a short walk from the tower.

To reach Tokyo Tower by bus, take the Toei Bus Hama 95 from Kamiyacho Station or Tokyo Station Marunouchi South Exit. Alternatively, consider the free shuttle bus route from Tokyo Station to Tokyo Tower.

To reach Tokyo Tower by walking from a general "Tokyo" location, the easiest approach is to take the JR Yamanote Line to Hamamatsucho Station, then walk about 15 minutes to the tower.

Local Specialties

Sushi

Traditional Japanese dish made with vinegared rice,typically seasoned with sugar and salt and combined with a variety of ingredients such as seafood, vegetables or meat.

Ramen

Popular Japanese noodle soup dish, typically featuring Chinese-style wheat noodles served in a meat- or fish-based broth, often flavored with soy sauce or miso and topped with various ingredients like sliced pork, seaweed and green onions.

Tempura

Japanese dish consisting of seafood or vegetables lightly battered and deep-fried until crispy,often served with a dipping sauce called tentsuyu.

Matcha

Type of powdered green tea made from finely ground, shade-grown tea leaves, typically whisked into hot water to create a frothy, vibrant green drink and is a staple in traditional Japanese tea ceremonies.
